What would you do if you lost your home? What can you do?

If you’re in a tough financial spot with your rent or mortgage, you might qualify for Pennsylvania housing choice vouchers, also known as Section 8 housing. Click this text to reveal more.

The Pennsylvania Housing Choice Voucher program, or Section 8, provides housing choice vouchers to eligible families and individuals who live in the state of Pennsylvania and are unable to afford safe and sanitary housing. The program is funded by the U.S Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD), and the local Public Housing Authority (PHA) administers the program locally to ensure that each applicant goes through a comprehensive eligibility test and meets Section 8 requirements before receiving subsidy. Step 1: Check your eligibility.

You are eligible for Section 8 housing if you:
A US citizen or non-citizen who has eligible immigration status.
Earning, as a family, less than 50% of the median income for the county or city in which you choose to live.
Meet other criteria based on assets and family composition.

You should be a US citizen or a qualified alien with all the necessary legal documentation to be considered for Section 8 housing.

Your household income should be at or below 50% of the area median income. HUD also publishes a extremely low income limit, usually set at 30% of the media income. PHAs will need to ensure 75% admissions in the this income category each year.

In order to be eligible for Section 8 program, you must not have been evicted from a Section 8 or another public housing, not have a drug-related criminal record, and meet other criteria based on assets and family composition.

Step 2: Document your income and housing costs.

When applying, you’ll need to show documentation for:
Your income.
Your housing cost.

Possible forms of verification include pay stubs from your employer, W-2 income statements, federal income tax returns, bank statements or unemployment statements.

This may be either your mortgage information or something in writing from your property owner that confirms your current rent.

Step 3: Know which voucher you need.

You can find a housing unit of your choice in the public housing market to rent or own. There are two types of vouchers:
Tenant-based vouchers.
Project-based vouchers.

Under a tenant-based voucher, you receive a voucher and can move into a unit with financial assistance. If you choose to move to another unit, the voucher carries over to the next unit, offering you continued assistance wherever you decide to live.

Under a project-based voucher, the owner of a complex receives the assistance directly. You receive assistance so long as you remain in the unit that the voucher was issued for. The voucher lasts for a specified unit and time. If you choose to leave the unit, the assistance does not carry over to the next unit.

Step 4: Apply for a voucher.

To begin the application process:
Locate your nearest PHA office.
Check if they are currently accepting applications.
Find out their office hours to complete the paperwork in person, or ask if you can complete the forms online.

Visit https://www.hud.gov/program_offices/public_indian_housing/pha/contacts/pa and search for the PHA in your city or county.

Your local PHA may have more applications than vouchers it can afford. If approved, you will be added to a waiting list.

But don't just wait!

Be persistent.
Finally, you can check your waitlist status at 412-520-3848.

Check if neighboring jurisdictions are open to applications. (If provided a voucher, you’ll have to move to that jurisdiction for at least 12 months, but you’re free to move after that.) Apply as early as possible. You might even apply to multiple locations to get your name on more than one waitlist.
